# Papers

Pages for the primary readings. Each page is a short guide to one paper: what it
is about, the background needed to follow it, and what to look for while reading.
It points you into the paper; it does not stand in for it.

| Paper | Section | Year | Summary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [madry-2018-pgd](madry-2018-pgd.md) | Adversarial Robustness of Classification Models | 2018 | The min-max robust-optimization framing of adversarial attacks and defenses; PGD and adversarial training. |
| [wei-2023-jailbroken](wei-2023-jailbroken.md) | Jailbreaking LLMs | 2023 | Two failure modes of LLM safety training, competing objectives and mismatched generalization, used to construct jailbreaks. |
| [greshake-2023-indirect-prompt-injection](greshake-2023-indirect-prompt-injection.md) | Indirect Prompt Injection in AI Agents | 2023 | Indirect prompt injection: untrusted retrieved content hijacks LLM-integrated applications; a security-perspective taxonomy and real-world demonstrations. |
| [qi-2024-shallow-safety-alignment](qi-2024-shallow-safety-alignment.md) | Safety Alignment and Guardrails | 2024 | "Shallow safety alignment": alignment that changes only the first few output tokens, why it explains many jailbreak and fine-tuning exploits, and how to deepen it. |
| [carlini-2022-lira](carlini-2022-lira.md) | Membership Inference Attacks | 2022 | Membership inference recast as a likelihood-ratio test (LiRA), and the case for evaluating attacks at low false-positive rates. |
| [carlini-2021-extracting-training-data](carlini-2021-extracting-training-data.md) | Training-data Extraction from LLMs | 2021 | A black-box training-data extraction attack on GPT-2: large LMs emit verbatim training sequences, including PII, even without overfitting. |
| [abadi-2016-dp-sgd](abadi-2016-dp-sgd.md) | Differential Privacy in ML | 2016 | DP-SGD and the moments accountant; training deep networks under a provable (epsilon, delta) guarantee. |
| [jang-2022-knowledge-unlearning](jang-2022-knowledge-unlearning.md) | Unlearning for Generative AI | 2022 | Knowledge unlearning: gradient ascent on target token sequences to make a deployed LM forget memorized data post-hoc, with empirical rather than formal privacy. |
| [orekondy-2019-knockoff-nets](orekondy-2019-knockoff-nets.md) | Model Extraction / Stealing | 2019 | Functionality stealing: training a "knockoff" image classifier from black-box query outputs alone, across different data distributions and architectures, including a commercial API for about $30. |
| [szyller-2019-dawn](szyller-2019-dawn.md) | Model Watermarking / Fingerprinting | 2019 | DAWN: watermarking model extraction by altering a small fraction of prediction-API responses, so the surrogate the adversary trains carries an owner-verifiable, client-specific backdoor. |
| [wang-2019-neural-cleanse](wang-2019-neural-cleanse.md) | Training-data Poisoning | 2019 | Neural Cleanse: detecting whether a trained classifier hides a backdoor by reverse-engineering a minimal trigger per label and flagging the outlier, then patching the model via input filtering, neuron pruning, and unlearning. |
| [zou-2024-poisonedrag](zou-2024-poisonedrag.md) | Preference Manipulation / RAG Poisoning | 2024 | PoisonedRAG: the first knowledge-corruption attack on RAG; injecting a few crafted texts into the retrieval corpus makes the system return an attacker-chosen answer to a target question, in black-box and white-box retriever settings. |
| [zou-2023-representation-engineering](zou-2023-representation-engineering.md) | Mechanistic Interpretability for AI safety | 2023 | Representation engineering (RepE): reading high-level concepts such as honesty as linear directions in a model's activations and steering behavior by manipulating them; a top-down approach to AI transparency. |
| [duddu-2024-unintended-interactions](duddu-2024-unintended-interactions.md) | Unintended Interactions among ML Defenses and Risks | 2024 | Overfitting and memorization as the conjectured common causes of unintended interactions among ML defenses and risks; a literature survey situating known interactions in one framework, a guideline to conjecture new ones, and two empirically validated examples. |
| [kirchenbauer-2023-llm-watermark](kirchenbauer-2023-llm-watermark.md) | Media Forensics and Proactive Provenance | 2023 | Watermarking language-model output at decoding time: a per-token green-list/red-list split softly biases sampling so the text carries a statistical signal, detectable with a z-test from the text alone, without the model or its API. |
| [pearce-2023-vulnerability-repair](pearce-2023-vulnerability-repair.md) | AI for Cybersecurity | 2023 | Zero-shot vulnerability repair with off-the-shelf code LLMs: prompting alone fixes every synthetic and hand-crafted CWE scenario, but reliability drops on real-world CVEs. |
| [zhang-2025-nexus](zhang-2025-nexus.md) | Leakage Resistance (Client Data): Software | 2025 | NEXUS: the first non-interactive (single-round) protocol for secure transformer inference; RNS-CKKS homomorphic encryption with SIMD ciphertext compression, slot folding, and a logarithmic-cost secure argmax, cutting bandwidth by orders of magnitude over interactive two-party baselines. |
| [elatali-2024-blime](elatali-2024-blime.md) | Leakage Resistance (Client Data): Hardware | 2024 | BliMe (Blinded Memory): RISC-V ISA extensions plus an attested hardware security module and encryption engine that mark a client's decrypted data "blinded" and enforce hardware taint tracking, so it cannot leak even through side channels or under attacker-controlled server software; with a machine-checked confidentiality proof. |
| [bao-2025-dp-zo](bao-2025-dp-zo.md) | Leakage Resistance (Training Data): Software | 2025 | DP-AggZO: differentially private fine-tuning of LLMs by zeroth-order (gradient-free) optimization; aggregating several forward-pass loss-difference estimates per record shrinks the clipping bias, matching or beating first-order DP-AdamW at a fraction of the memory. |
| [zhang-2024-tee-shielded](zhang-2024-tee-shielded.md) | Leakage Resistance (Training Data): Hardware | 2024 | An (in-)security study of TEE-Shielded DNN Partition (TSDP): benchmarking how much on-device model and training-data privacy leaks through the GPU-offloaded part of a partitioned DNN, with model stealing and membership inference, and a partition-before-training defense (TeeSlice) that matches whole-model shielding at over 10x less TEE cost. |
| [tang-2024-modelguard](tang-2024-modelguard.md) | Model Theft Resistance (+ On-Device Private Inference): Software | 2024 | ModelGuard: a prediction-perturbation defense against model extraction, posed as one constrained optimization over the returned confidence vectors that covers both parameter-stealing and functionality-stealing; an information-theoretic variant minimizes the mutual information between clean and returned predictions to bound what an adaptive, Bayes-optimal attacker can recover. |
| [moon-2025-asgard](moon-2025-asgard.md) | Model Theft Resistance (+ On-Device Private Inference): Hardware | 2025 | ASGARD: the first virtualization-based TEE for on-device DNN protection on legacy Armv8-A SoCs; a VM enclave extended over an integrated NPU through secure I/O passthrough holds the whole model, keeping the trusted computing base small and run-time overhead near zero without modifying proprietary accelerator drivers or the EL3 secure monitor. |
| [qu-2025-zkgpt](qu-2025-zkgpt.md) | Regulatory Compliance: Software | 2025 | zkGPT: a non-interactive zero-knowledge proof system for transformer (GPT-2) inference; tailored sumcheck/GKR proofs and lookup arguments for the linear and non-linear layers let a provider prove an output is the correct result of a committed model, hiding the weights, in under 25 seconds with proofs near 100 KB. |
| [chantasantitam-2026-palm](chantasantitam-2026-palm.md) | Regulatory Compliance: Hardware | 2026 | PAL\*M: the first hardware-assisted property-attestation framework for large generative models; a confidential VM (Intel TDX) and a TEE-aware GPU (NVIDIA H100) measure CPU-GPU operations while an incremental multiset hash tracks the integrity of memory-mapped datasets sampled from untrusted storage, letting a provider attest training, fine-tuning, evaluation, and chat-inference properties to a regulator without revealing the model or data; protocol machine-checked in Tamarin, under 11% overhead on common operations. |

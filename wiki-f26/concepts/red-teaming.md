# Red teaming (language models)

## Definition

Red teaming deliberately probes a model for outputs that violate its safety
policy, both to surface weaknesses before deployment and to generate data the
model is then trained to refuse. The probing prompts may be written by humans or
generated by another model (Perez et al., 2022; Ganguli et al., 2022).
Red-teaming datasets double as evaluation benchmarks: prompts the model creators
judged harmful and trained against are a stringent test, since any attack that
still succeeds on them has defeated the cases safety training targeted directly.
